arc_id: arc-beginner-kicking-94b252e5236b   Level: Beginner   Theme: Rear round kick: stance through step-in check-and-return

Terminal goal: By the end of Night 3, students can step in with a rear round kick, recover in balance, check a partner's controlled counter kick, and return immediately with a round kick.

Progression: Night 1 teaches the base rear round kick in place. Night 2 adds a step-in entry so the same kick closes distance. Night 3 adds checking a partner's counter kick and returning immediately, assembling all three nights into the arc's terminal drill. Each night begins (from Night 2 onward) with a compressed review of the prior night's terminal drill so a student who missed a night can still participate. Each night runs Warm Up (8) -> New and Exciting (12) -> Muay Thai Curriculum (35) -> Burnout (5): an endorphin sandwich -- energetic open, focused technical teaching, hard effort close.

Generation warnings

  • Night 3: Curriculum uses a 4th item (at the beginner cap).
  • Night 3: rung 'Check mechanics (live, portion only)' has only 7 min -- unusually little repetition time for a primary stage.

Why these sources were selected

Easton's own kickboxing curriculum carries the whole ladder (Stance, Round Kicks) because it's the only pool written as an actual instructor script with explicit common-errors and an explicit note on where a variant (the switch kick) becomes harder than this class needs. FightClub supplies the terminal pad application. Warm Up, New and Exciting, and Burnout are arc-constructed from general athletic movement, the round kick's own footwork/hip cues, and a standard bodyweight interval finisher, specifically to open and close the class at high energy (endorphin sandwich) without assuming any untaught skill.
