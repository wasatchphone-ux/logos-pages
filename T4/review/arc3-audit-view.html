Terminal goal: By the end of Night 3, students can chain a jab-cross into a teep, on shields, with clean distance control.

Progression: Night 1 teaches the lead teep in isolation, connected to its role as a distance-management tool (the 'foot jab' comparison). Night 2 adds the rear teep and combines both into an alternating rhythm. Night 3 chains the teep onto Arc 2's jab-cross combination, assembling all three nights into the arc's terminal drill: jab-cross into a teep, on shields, with clean distance control. Each night begins (from Night 2 onward) with a compressed review of the prior terminal skill so a student who missed a night can still participate. Each night runs Warm Up (8) -> New and Exciting (12) -> Muay Thai Curriculum (35) -> Burnout (5): an endorphin sandwich -- energetic open, focused technical teaching, hard effort close.
