Night 1 -- Jab Foundation

Level: Beginner
Objective: Throw a clean, balanced jab with a fast return to guard.
Equipment checklist: 10 focus mitts, Boxing gloves (required for the striking partner in every partner punching drill tonight)
Format: Partner pairs required
Space: Open floor. 4 rows of 5 for Warm Up and the New and Exciting mitt-touch relay. 10 partner pairs spread across the floor for the mitt terminal drill.
Safety:
  • Jab mechanics: Use only the jab-specific mechanics from this source (hand leads from face, hip pop, violent retraction, exhale) -- the cross/hook/uppercut portions of this same video are taught in later nights and arcs.

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Mitt-target jab-count relay
  • Light, constrained jab touches into a partner-held focus mitt, counted for a friendly competitive total.
Setup:
A: Does light, constrained jab touches into the mitt Partner B holds -- a tap/touch, not a full-power strike -- for a timed 30-second round, counting reps out loud.
B: Holds a focus mitt at face height, braced, calling out the running count with Partner A.
Reps: Round 1 stance-and-jab touch, round 2 moving mitt targets, round 3 45-second accuracy challenge per role.
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch immediately: holder becomes toucher, toucher becomes holder.
Reset: Reset to guard after every touch or miss.
Equipment: focus mitt
Touch contact in New and Exciting.

Muay Thai Curriculum 35 min

10 min Jab mechanics
  • Pop the hip forward for the jab without a big step or foot turn
  • Hand leads from the face
  • Retract punch violently back to face
Setup:
Reps: 2-minute teach, 3 x 8 slow jabs, 3 x 6 jab-and-reset reps, then 60-second coach-call jab accuracy challenge.
Sides: jab is thrown with the lead hand only; whichever stance is the student's natural one (orthodox or southpaw) -- no switching mid-arc
Use only the jab-specific mechanics from this source (hand leads from face, hip pop, violent retraction, exhale) -- the cross/hook/uppercut portions of this same video are taught in later nights and arcs.
10 min Jab footwork -- moving forward and angling
  • Drive off the back leg as the motor for movement
  • Push twice, once for each punch
  • Back foot must come with you after each jab to avoid overextension
Setup:
Reps: 2-minute footwork teach, 4 forward double-jab entries, 4 angle entries, 4 entry-and-reset reps, 60-second line-entry challenge.
Sides: jab thrown with the lead hand; stepping/angling direction tied to the student's natural lead side
15 min Terminal mitt drill -- jab on focus mitts, 10 pairs
  • Same mechanics as the solo reps, now on mitts
  • Reset to guard after every rep
  • 50% power -- technical focus, not power focus
Setup:
A: Strikes first. Throws a jab into the focus mitt Partner B holds. Resets to full guard after every single jab before throwing the next.
B: Holds one focus mitt at face height with both hands, elbows in. Calls 'ready' before each rep. Resets mitt position between reps.
Reps: Set 1 plain jab, set 2 jab after step-in, set 3 jab after angle, final 45-second clean-jab count per student.
Switch: After Partner A completes round 1 (10 jabs), partners switch immediately: B becomes striker, A becomes holder, for B's round 1. Repeat for round 2.
Reset: Reset to guard and range after every jab.
Equipment: 1 focus mitt per pair (10 mitts total for a class of 20)
50% cap; holder calls ready.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Jab-volume mitt burst
  • Hard-effort jab-volume finisher on the mitts already in use tonight.
Setup:
A: Jabs the mitt Partner B holds for 30 seconds of maximum controlled volume each round.
B: Holds the mitt braced for Partner A's 30-second work interval, then the roles switch.
Reps: 4 rounds x 30s work per role (2 minutes of jabbing work per student total)
Switch: Every 30 seconds, immediate switch (7-8 second transition).
Reset: n/a -- continuous work within each round, full stop only at the end of the 5 minutes.
Equipment: 1 focus mitt per pair (the same mitts used in tonight's New and Exciting drill).
Format: 4 rounds of 75 seconds, exactly 5 minutes. Each round: Partner A jabs the mitt for 30s max controlled volume, quick 7-second switch, Partner B jabs the mitt for 30s max controlled volume, quick 8-second switch/reset, next round begins immediately.
Coach calls: Coach runs a repeating 75-second timer, calls 'SWITCH' at each interval, counts down the last 10 seconds of the final round.
Scaling: Scale from full-speed jabs to a slower, fully-extended controlled pace for students whose shoulders fatigue early.
Jabs stay technically controlled even at speed -- no wild, off-balance strikes. Mitt holder must brace fully every rep; coach can call any student to slow down if form degrades.

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Reactive target-call jab-cross relay
  • React to a partner's unpredictable 'jab' or 'cross' call by throwing the correct punch into the held mitt.
Setup:
A: Reacts. Listens for the coach-style call from Partner B ('jab' or 'cross', unpredictable order) and throws the correct light touch into the mitt.
B: Holds a focus mitt and calls 'jab' or 'cross' at an unpredictable cadence, presenting the mitt for whichever punch is called.
Reps: Round 1 fixed jab-cross, round 2 high/low target calls, round 3 45-second clean-call challenge per role.
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch roles: caller becomes the reactor.
Reset: Reset to guard after each combination.
Equipment: focus mitt
Targets only; holder must be set.

8 min Cross mechanics
  • Step into the punch as you throw it
  • Target the head/face -- this arc's beginner default
  • Keep the throwing-side shoulder up high to protect the chin
Setup:
Reps: 90-second teach, 3 x 6 slow crosses, 3 x 4 cross-and-reset reps, 45-second coach-call cross challenge.
Sides: cross is thrown with the rear hand only; whichever stance is the student's natural one
8 min Jab-cross combination mechanics
  • Jab to distract or occupy the guard, then follow with the cross to the head
  • Return to a good stance after each combination
Setup:
Reps: 90-second teach, 3 x 6 jab-crosses, 3 x 4 jab-cross-reset reps, 45-second rhythm challenge.
Sides: jab lead hand, cross rear hand, whichever is the student's natural stance
Use only the jab-then-cross portion of this source. It also demonstrates hook and uppercut setups to the same cross -- those are optional-exposure/deferred material, not this arc's required combination.
14 min Terminal pad drill -- jab-cross combination, 10 pairs
  • Shoulder up on the punch, good extension on the jab
  • Default: both punches to the head. Variation: jab to the head, cross to the body
  • Return to stance after the combination
Setup:
A: Strikes first. Throws jab-cross combinations into the mitts Partner B holds -- first a head-level pair (this arc's default), then jab-to-head/cross-to-body as a target-variety variation. Returns to stance after every combo.
B: Holds two focus mitts (one head height, one body height), braced, calling 'ready' before each rep.
Reps: Set 1 fixed jab-cross, set 2 target-call jab-cross, set 3 jab-cross after step-in, final clean-volume challenge per student.
Switch: After Partner A completes round 1 (8 combos), partners switch immediately: B becomes striker, A becomes holder, for B's round 1. Repeat for round 2.
Reset: Reset to range after every combination.
Equipment: 2 focus mitts per pair, 20 mitts total for a class of 20 pairs.
50% cap; mitts set before every rep.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Squat-jump + punch-out interval
  • Hard-effort solo interval finisher, no equipment, no partner.
Setup:
Reps: 5 rounds x 40s work (self-paced effort each interval)
Sides: punch-out rounds alternate jab-cross symmetrically, no side distinction
Format: 5 rounds of 40 seconds work / 20 seconds rest, exactly 5 minutes. Rounds 1, 3, 5: squat jumps. Rounds 2, 4: alternating shadow jab-cross punch-outs at maximum controlled speed.
Coach calls: Coach runs a visible 40/20 interval timer, calls 'GO' at the start of each work interval and 'REST' at each rest interval, counts down the last 5 seconds of the final round.
Scaling: Squat jumps scale down to bodyweight squats (no jump) for lower-impact students; punch-outs scale down to a slower, fully-extended controlled pace for students whose shoulders fatigue early.
Land softly with soft knees on every jump; self-pace intensity; punches stay technically controlled even at speed -- no wild, off-balance strikes.

Night 3 -- Movement, Distance and Reaction

Level: Beginner
Objective: Throw a clean jab-cross combination while moving into appropriate distance, on pads, and recover to a balanced stance.
Equipment checklist: 10 focus mitts, Boxing gloves (required for the striking partner in every partner punching drill tonight)
Format: Partner pairs required
Space: Open floor. 4 rows of 5 for Warm Up. 10 partner pairs with mitts for New and Exciting and all Curriculum rungs. Personal floor lines for the solo footwork rung.
Safety:
  • Reactive jab-cross -- constrained give-and-take: Use only the jab-cross opening portion of this drill's progressive build (start with one punch at a time, then jab-cross) -- the hook-hook extension used later in this same source is optional-exposure/deferred material for a future arc.
  • Terminal assembled drill -- step in, jab-cross, on pads, recover to stance: Constructed terminal drill -- no single source contains this complete sequence; component sources are cited above and only their beginner-appropriate portions are used (Give & Take Drill's jab-cross opening only, not its hook-hook extension).

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Distance-call jab-cross entry game
  • React to a partner stepping the mitt back by closing the new distance with a step, then throwing a light jab-cross touch.
Setup:
A: Reacts. On the coach's call, steps to close the new distance the mitt just opened, then lightly touches a jab-cross into it.
B: Holds a focus mitt and, on the coach's call, takes one step back to open the distance, then holds still for Partner A's reaction.
Reps: Round 1 near/far entry calls, round 2 side target calls, round 3 45-second clean-entry challenge per role.
Switch: After 8 reactions, partners switch roles: holder becomes the reactor, reactor becomes the holder.
Reset: A exits outside range after every jab-cross.
Equipment: focus mitt
Touch contact only in New and Exciting.

Muay Thai Curriculum 35 min

5 min Compressed review: jab-cross combination
  • Same combination as Night 2: jab, then cross, return to stance
Setup:
A: Throws 6 jab-cross combinations into the mitt Partner B holds, returning to stance after each.
B: Holds one focus mitt, calls 'ready' before each rep.
Reps: 6 combinations per student (A throws 6, switch, B throws 6).
Switch: After A's 6 combos, partners switch immediately for B's 6.
Reset: Striker returns to stance after every combination.
Equipment: 1 focus mitt per pair.
50% power cap; mitt holder calls 'ready' before each rep.
8 min Footwork with punches -- L-Step and Pivot Step
  • Lead leg comes back to rear leg, rear leg goes out to create an L shape, step back into the strike position
  • Step lead leg out to get head off the center line, pivot around to create angle, keep it subtle
Setup:
Reps: Teach L-step and pivot, then 3 L-step jab-cross reps, 3 pivot jab-cross reps, 4 coach-call angle exits, 45-second reset challenge.
Sides: whichever is the student's natural lead
8 min Reactive jab-cross -- constrained give-and-take
  • Jab down the middle, cross down the middle
  • Blocker: hand in front for the jab, turn and cover for the cross
  • Start one punch at a time before combining
Setup:
A: Throws jab, then cross, onto Partner B's guard at controlled pace, placing shots down the middle of the guard.
B: Blocks: hand in front of the face for the jab, turns and covers for the cross, staying comfortable with contact coming at the guard.
Reps: Set 1 fixed jab-cross to guard, set 2 receiver gives one movement cue, set 3 jab-cross and reset race per role.
Switch: After A's 10 reps, partners switch immediately for B's 10.
Reset: Both partners reset after each give-and-take rep.
Equipment: None.
Constrained, predictable reaction only.
14 min Terminal assembled drill -- step in, jab-cross, on pads, recover to stance
  • Close the distance with a clean step, don't just lean in
  • Jab-cross into the mitt the instant you arrive
  • Recover to a full, balanced stance -- ready for whatever comes next
Setup:
A: Reacts to Partner B's 'go' call: closes the new distance with an L-step or pivot step, throws a jab-cross combination into the mitt the instant they arrive, then recovers to a full, balanced stance.
B: Holds the focus mitt, calls 'go' at an unpredictable moment, and steps to open a new distance before each call so Partner A must genuinely close the gap rather than punch from a fixed spot.
Reps: Set 1 step-in jab-cross, set 2 step-in jab-cross and exit, set 3 moving target, final clean assembled challenge per student.
Switch: After Partner A completes 6 full sequences, partners switch roles for B's 6 sequences. Repeat for round 2.
Reset: A must recover to balanced stance before the next feed.
Equipment: 1 focus mitt per pair (already in hand from the compressed review -- no new equipment needed).
50% cap into mitts.
