New and Exciting structure

12 min — Partner shield touch-count relay
ObjectiveLight, constrained knee-lift-and-touch reps into a partner-held shield, counted for a friendly competitive total.
Why it fits tonightReinforces step angle, hip turn, balance and target awareness with real partner feedback and a competitive count, so it feels different from the technical Warm Up and doesn't front-run Curriculum's full-power pad drill.
Engagement featurespartner_interaction, equipment, timing, competition, target_work, immediate_feedback
Station formation10 pairs, spaced across the open floor, all working simultaneously
Throughput justificationEvery pair works simultaneously with its own shield; no shared equipment, no line to wait in.
Conflicts with Curriculum?none
Partner aDoes light, constrained knee-lift-and-touch reps into the shield Partner B holds -- a tap/touch, not a full-power strike -- for a timed 30-second round, counting reps out loud.
Partner bHolds a kicking shield low at hip/thigh height, braced, calling out the running count with Partner A.
Equipment1 kicking shield per pair (10 shields for a class of 20).
Contact levelLight touch/tap only, constrained movement -- not a full-power strike. Full-power technique is taught later in Curriculum.
Reps3 rounds of 30 seconds per role (touches counted each round)
Rounds3
Switch pointAfter each 30-second round, partners switch immediately: holder becomes toucher, toucher becomes holder.
Reset conditionReset to athletic base between individual touches; full reset to starting position at the top of each new round.
Coaching paceCoach starts/stops each 30-second round on a visible clock and calls out the switch.
Safety limitsTouch/tap contact only -- shield holder must brace before each round begins; no full-power strikes.

New and Exciting structure

12 min — Step-to-target reaction drill
ObjectiveReact to a partner stepping back by closing the new distance and lightly touching the shield.
Why it fits tonightTurns forward entry into a live reactive game with a partner and a real target -- distance changes unpredictably, so students must actually react and connect the step to a touch, which is exactly tonight's theme, but with real feedback instead of solo rehearsal.
Engagement featurespartner_interaction, reaction, equipment, timing, target_work, immediate_feedback
Station formation10 pairs, spaced across the open floor, all working simultaneously
Throughput justificationEvery pair works simultaneously with its own shield; no shared equipment, no line to wait in.
Conflicts with Curriculum?none
Partner aReacts. On the coach's call, steps in with the lead foot to close the new distance and lightly touches the shield with a knee-lift-to-target (light touch, not a full kick).
Partner bHolds a kicking shield and, on the coach's call, takes one step back to open the distance, then holds still for Partner A's reaction.
Equipment1 kicking shield per pair (10 shields for a class of 20).
Contact levelLight touch/tap only, constrained movement -- full-power technique is taught on pads in Curriculum.
Reps3 rounds of 8 reactions per role
Rounds3
Switch pointAfter 8 reactions, partners switch roles: holder becomes the reactor, reactor becomes the holder.
Reset conditionFull reset to starting distance after each reaction before the next call.
Coaching paceCoach calls 'ready... STEP' at an unpredictable cadence to keep the reaction genuine.
Safety limitsTouch/tap contact only; shield holder must be set and stable before calling for the next rep.
