Burnout (final 5 minutes)

5 min — Jab-cross-teep volume burst partner
Why (this class)A hand-speed-and-balance finisher using tonight's exact combination (jab-cross-teep) at maximum volume, closing the class at high effort.
Exact format4 rounds of 75 seconds, exactly 5 minutes. Each round is split into two 15-second phases so the target holder never has to hold the mitt and the kicking shield at once: Partner A jab-crosses the mitt for 15s max controlled volume, Partner B sets the mitt down and braces the shield with both hands, Partner A teeps the shield for 15s max controlled volume, quick 7-second switch, Partner B repeats both phases for 30s, quick 8-second switch/reset, next round begins immediately.
Coach call structureCoach runs a repeating 75-second timer with a mid-round 15-second call for the mitt-to-shield transition, calls 'SWITCH' at each partner interval, counts down the last 10 seconds of the final round.
Scaling optionScale from full-speed combinations to a slower, fully-controlled pace for students whose balance or shoulders fatigue early.
Safety limitCombinations stay technically controlled even at speed -- no wild, off-balance strikes. Target holder must brace fully every rep, and must have the shield fully braced with both hands before any teep is thrown at it.
Equal work / switchingyes -- Both partners get exactly 30 seconds of work in every round -- work is equal by construction.
