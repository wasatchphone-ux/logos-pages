Night 1 -- Stance, Guard and Balance

Level: Beginner
Objective: Hold stance and guard while moving and resetting.
Equipment checklist: open floor only
Format: Partner pairs required
Space: Open floor, 4 rows of 5, then 10 partner lanes for reaction work.

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Mirror stance reaction game
  • Partner mirrors stance, guard and small steps so foundation feels live without strikes.
Setup:
A: Mirrors B for one round, then adds a light jab-touch to B's open palm after each clean freeze.
B: Leads small steps in round 1, changes direction on coach calls in round 2, and presents an open-palm target after clean freezes in round 3.
Reps: Round 1 mirror only, round 2 coach-call direction changes, round 3 freeze-and-jab-touch challenge; switch roles each round.
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch roles.
Reset: Both reset to stance after every freeze or target touch.
Equipment: open floor lanes
Touch target only; no power punching.

Muay Thai Curriculum 35 min

12 min Muay Thai stance and guard
  • Feet under hips, stance long enough to move
  • Hands high, elbows in
  • Soft knees and eyes forward
Setup:
Reps: Brief stance teach, 3 x 30-second guard holds, 3 x 30-second coach-call jab-touch freezes, then 60-second balance challenge.
Sides: natural stance unless otherwise stated
Self-contained teach; keep only the listed beginner portion of the source.
10 min Basic movement: forward, back and lateral
  • Lead with the foot in the direction you move
  • Small steps
  • Do not cross feet
Setup:
Reps: Teach the step pattern, then 4 call-and-move lanes, 4 move-and-jab-touch reps, and a final 60-second direction-call challenge.
Sides: natural stance unless otherwise stated
Self-contained teach; keep only the listed beginner portion of the source.
13 min Terminal partner balance-reaction drill
  • Move on cue
  • Freeze in guard
  • Reset before the next cue
Setup:
A: Starts in guard. On B's hand cue, takes one clean step in the shown direction, freezes in guard, then resets.
B: Feeds one clear cue at a time: palm forward means step back, palm pulled back means step in, palm left/right means lateral step.
Reps: 2 rounds of 8 cue-and-step reps per student.
Switch: After Partner A finishes the assigned reps, partners switch roles; repeat for round 2.
Reset: Both partners reset to the center mark after every cue.
Equipment: open floor lanes
No trick cues; first-day students get slower cues.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Solo footwork sprint intervals
  • Close class with controlled high intensity.
Setup:
Reps: 5 rounds of 40 seconds fast feet / 20 seconds rest.
Sides: symmetric
Format: 5 rounds of 40 seconds fast feet / 20 seconds rest.
Coach calls: Coach runs the clock, calls every work/rest/switch interval, and counts down the final 10 seconds.
Scaling: Reduce speed, remove jumps, or shorten the strike burst while keeping the same interval structure.
Technique stays controlled under fatigue; students stop if dizzy or unable to hold form.

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Coach-call compass step race
  • Students react to north/south/east/west calls, then freeze in guard.
Setup:
A: Moves one square on the coach call, resets, then adds a jab-touch to B's palm when the coach calls target.
B: Watches stance in the first round, gives side-pressure cues in the second, and presents a palm target in the final challenge round.
Reps: Round 1 direction calls, round 2 pressure-cue resets, round 3 direction plus jab-touch race; switch roles each round.
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch roles.
Reset: Return to the center mark after every call.
Equipment: open floor lanes
Small steps and touch targets only.

10 min Terminal mirror movement with pivot reset
  • Small steps
  • No crossing feet
  • Freeze balanced
Setup:

B: Mirrors the same three moves. If B loses stance, both reset and repeat slower.
Reps: 2 rounds of 5 three-move patterns per leader.
Switch: After Partner A finishes the assigned reps, partners switch roles; repeat for round 2.
Reset: Both partners reset to facing stance after each three-move pattern.
Equipment: open floor lanes
No contact and no racing through the pivot.

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Partner tag-without-tag footwork game
  • Partner changes distance with no contact; mover maintains range and freezes on call.
Setup:
A: Keeps the same distance while B drifts forward, back or sideways inside the movement box.
B: Moves like a slow tagger without touching. Changes direction every 2-3 steps to test A's balance.
Reps: 3 rounds of 30 seconds per role.
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch roles.
Reset: Coach call freeze means both students stop in guard, check distance, and restart.
Equipment: open floor lanes
No touching, no sprinting, stay inside the box.

Sides: natural stance unless otherwise stated

10 min Partner distance management
  • Match distance
  • Do not chase
  • Reset when crowded
Setup:
A: Keeps range while B pressures, then touches B's palm with jab when the distance is correct.
B: Walks forward, back or sideways at coach pace and presents a palm target only when A finds range.
Reps: Round 1 hold range, round 2 range plus jab-touch, round 3 45-second distance challenge per role.
Switch: After Partner A finishes the assigned reps, partners switch roles; repeat for round 2.
Reset: Both reset to arm's length after every target touch.
Equipment: open floor lanes
Walking pace, touch target only.
20 min Terminal foundation application: cue, move, reset
  • See the cue
  • Move one clean step
  • Reset in guard
Setup:
A: Answers B's movement cue, touches the open palm with jab or jab-cross, then exits to stance.
B: Feeds one movement at a time, presents palm targets after clean movement, then freezes for A's reset.
Reps: Round 1 cue-and-move, round 2 cue-move-jab, round 3 cue-move-jab-cross-reset challenge.
Switch: After Partner A finishes the assigned reps, partners switch roles; repeat for round 2.
Reset: After each exchange, both return to arm's length in guard.
Equipment: open floor lanes
Touch targets only; no sparring or chasing.
