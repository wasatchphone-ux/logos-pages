Night 1 -- Stance, Guard and Balance

Level: Beginner
Objective: Hold stance and guard while moving and resetting.
Equipment checklist: open floor only
Format: Partner pairs required
Space: Open floor, 4 rows of 5, then 10 partner lanes for reaction work.

Warm Up 8 min

Warm-up menu: use these six-plus options, combine or trim based on the room while keeping the block exactly 8 minutes.

1.0 min Jumping jacks
  • Raise heart rate quickly.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: symmetric unless the coach assigns stance side
1.0 min High knees in place
  • Build heat and knee drive.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: symmetric unless the coach assigns stance side
1.0 min Fast feet
  • Prime light feet.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: symmetric unless the coach assigns stance side
1.0 min Bodyweight squats
  • Warm hips and legs.
Setup:
Reps: 12 controlled reps
Sides: symmetric unless the coach assigns stance side
1.0 min Line hops
  • Add direction changes without technique load.
Setup:
Reps: 30 seconds forward/back, 30 seconds lateral
Sides: symmetric unless the coach assigns stance side
1.0 min Foundation base check
  • Find stance, guard and balance before class-specific work.
Setup:
Reps: 3 holds of 5 seconds
Sides: symmetric unless the coach assigns stance side
1.0 min Foundation movement primer
  • Preview the movement path used in Curriculum.
Setup:
Reps: 8 slow reps
Sides: symmetric unless the coach assigns stance side
1.0 min Reset-to-stance check
  • Make recovery to stance automatic.
Setup:
Reps: 6 resets on coach clap
Sides: symmetric unless the coach assigns stance side

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Mirror stance reaction game
  • Partner mirrors stance, guard and small steps so foundation feels live without strikes.
Setup:
A: Works the reaction task at light contact or no contact, staying inside the night's skill limit.
B: Feeds the visual cue, target, or distance change exactly as coached, then holds still for the response.
Reps: 3 rounds of 30 seconds per role
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch roles.
Reset: Reset to full stance after each cue before the next cue.
Equipment: open floor lanes
No surprise full-power contact; the feeder must be set before the response.

Muay Thai Curriculum 35 min

12 min Muay Thai stance and guard
  • Feet under hips, stance long enough to move
  • Hands high, elbows in
  • Soft knees and eyes forward
Setup:
Reps: 5 sets of 20-second stance holds
Sides: natural stance unless otherwise stated
Self-contained teach; keep only the listed beginner portion of the source.
10 min Basic movement: forward, back and lateral
  • Lead with the foot in the direction you move
  • Small steps
  • Do not cross feet
Setup:
Reps: 4 lanes each direction
Sides: natural stance unless otherwise stated
Self-contained teach; keep only the listed beginner portion of the source.
13 min Terminal partner balance-reaction drill
  • Move on cue
  • Freeze in guard
  • Reset before the next cue
Setup:
A: Performs the active skill sequence, then resets to stance before the next rep.
B: Feeds or holds the target exactly as assigned, then resets before the next rep.
Reps: 2 rounds of 6-8 clean reps per student, adjusted by coach pace
Switch: After Partner A finishes the assigned reps, partners switch roles; repeat for round 2.
Reset: Both partners reset to full stance after every rep or exchange.
Equipment: open floor lanes
Beginner pace only; no escalation beyond clean, controlled timing.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Solo footwork sprint intervals
  • Close class with controlled high intensity.
Setup:
Reps: 5 rounds of 40 seconds fast feet / 20 seconds rest.
Sides: symmetric
Format: 5 rounds of 40 seconds fast feet / 20 seconds rest.
Coach calls: Coach runs the clock, calls every work/rest/switch interval, and counts down the final 10 seconds.
Scaling: Reduce speed, remove jumps, or shorten the strike burst while keeping the same interval structure.
Technique stays controlled under fatigue; students stop if dizzy or unable to hold form.
