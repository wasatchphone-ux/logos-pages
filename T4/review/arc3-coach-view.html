Night 1 -- Lead Teep Foundation

Level: Beginner
Objective: Throw a clean, balanced lead teep with a controlled return to stance.
Equipment checklist: 10 kicking shields
Format: Partner pairs required
Space: Open floor. 4 rows of 5 for Warm Up and the New and Exciting balance-target relay. 10 partner pairs spread across the floor for the shield terminal drill.
Safety:
  • Lead teep mechanics: Use only the lead-teep mechanics from this source -- the feint and combination-setup content later in this same video is taught in a later night/arc.

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Balance-hold target relay
  • Light, constrained knee-chamber-and-touch reps into a partner-held kicking shield, counted for a friendly competitive total.
Setup:
A: Does light, constrained knee-lift-and-touch reps into the kicking shield Partner B holds -- a tap/touch, not a full-power strike -- for a timed 30-second round, counting reps out loud.
B: Holds a kicking shield at body height, braced, calling out the running count with Partner A.
Reps: 3 rounds of 30 seconds per role
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch immediately: holder becomes toucher, toucher becomes holder.
Reset: Reset to athletic base between individual touches; full reset to starting position at the top of each new round.
Equipment: kicking shield
Touch/tap contact only -- shield holder must brace before each round begins; no full-power strikes.

12 min Lead teep mechanics
  • Shift weight to rear foot so it is directly under hip
  • Bring lead knee up towards chest, getting it nice and high
  • Drive hips forward with hinging motion to generate power
Setup:
Reps: 5 sets of 6 teeps, reset to stance between every teep
Sides: lead leg teeps only; whichever stance is the student's natural one (orthodox or southpaw) -- no switching mid-arc
Use only the lead-teep mechanics from this source -- the feint and combination-setup content later in this same video is taught in a later night/arc.
8 min Teep as a range tool -- the foot jab comparison
  • Compare the teep to a jab -- a 'foot jab' for distance and disruption
  • Opposite hand stays tight while teeping
  • Counter-balance with the same side arm
Setup:
Reps: 4 sets of 6 teeps, focused on range/timing rather than power
Sides: lead leg
15 min Terminal shield drill -- lead teep on kicking shields, 10 pairs
  • Same four-part mechanics as the solo reps, now on the shield
  • Reset to stance after every rep
  • 50% power -- technical focus, not power focus
Setup:
A: Strikes first. Throws a lead teep into the kicking shield Partner B holds. Resets to full stance after every single teep before throwing the next.
B: Holds one kicking shield at body height with both hands, braced on the back leg. Calls 'ready' before each rep.
Reps: 2 rounds of 8 teeps per student (16 teeps per student total)
Switch: After Partner A completes round 1 (8 teeps), partners switch immediately: B becomes striker, A becomes holder, for B's round 1. Repeat for round 2.
Reset: Striker resets to full stance after every individual teep; pair resets to neutral facing position before each new round.
Equipment: 1 kicking shield per pair (10 shields total for a class of 20)
50% power cap; shield holder must call 'ready' before each rep so the striker never teeps a target that isn't set.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Teep-volume shield burst
  • Hard-effort lead-teep-volume finisher on the kicking shields already in use tonight.
Setup:
A: Teeps the kicking shield Partner B holds for 30 seconds of maximum controlled volume each round.
B: Holds the shield braced for Partner A's 30-second work interval, then the roles switch.
Reps: 4 rounds x 30s work per role
Switch: Every 30 seconds, immediate switch (7-8 second transition).
Reset: n/a -- continuous work within each round, full stop only at the end of the 5 minutes.
Equipment: 1 kicking shield per pair (the same shields used in tonight's New and Exciting drill).
Format: 4 rounds of 75 seconds, exactly 5 minutes. Each round: Partner A teeps the shield for 30s max controlled volume, quick 7-second switch, Partner B teeps the shield for 30s max controlled volume, quick 8-second switch/reset, next round begins immediately.
Coach calls: Coach runs a repeating 75-second timer, calls 'SWITCH' at each interval, counts down the last 10 seconds of the final round.
Scaling: Scale from full-speed teeps to a slower, fully-controlled balance-focused pace for students whose balance fatigues early.
Teeps stay technically controlled even at speed -- no wild, off-balance kicks. Shield holder must brace fully every rep.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Single-leg balance squat + high-knee interval
  • Hard-effort solo interval finisher, no equipment, no partner, with a balance component that stays on-theme for a teep arc.
Setup:
Reps: 5 rounds x 40s work (self-paced effort each interval)
Sides: high-knee rounds alternate legs symmetrically, no side distinction
Format: 5 rounds of 40 seconds work / 20 seconds rest, exactly 5 minutes. Rounds 1, 3, 5: bodyweight squats. Rounds 2, 4: alternating high knees at maximum controlled cadence.
Coach calls: Coach runs a visible 40/20 interval timer, calls 'GO' at the start of each work interval and 'REST' at each rest interval, counts down the last 5 seconds of the final round.
Scaling: Squats scale down to a shallower range of motion for students with knee sensitivity; high knees scale down to a slower marching cadence for students whose balance fatigues early.
Land softly with soft knees; self-pace intensity; stop and reset balance if wobbling rather than forcing through it.

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Combination-call reaction relay
  • React to a partner's unpredictable 'hands' or 'teep' call by throwing the correct light touch into the matching target.
Setup:
A: Reacts. Listens for the call ('hands' or 'teep', unpredictable order) and throws the correct light touch into the matching target.
B: Keeps a focus mitt on one hand and a kicking shield propped ready at their feet, calling 'hands' or 'teep' at an unpredictable cadence -- raises the mitt for a 'hands' call, or drops the mitt hand and scoops the shield up with both hands, bracing it, for a 'teep' call.
Reps: 3 rounds of 30 seconds per role
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch roles: caller becomes the reactor.
Reset: Reset to guard/stance after every reaction before the next call.
Equipment: focus mitt and kicking shield
Touch/tap contact only; holder must be set and stable before calling the next target.

10 min Teep as a combination finisher
  • Use jab and teep combinations to practice together
  • Practice lead teep and push kick setup combinations
  • Retraction and reset is the most critical part -- don't skip it under combination speed
Setup:
Reps: 5 sets of 6 jab-cross-teep combinations
Sides: jab lead hand, cross rear hand, teep lead leg, whichever is the student's natural stance
Use only the jab-teep combination-practice guidance from this source -- its other content (rear teep mechanics, alternating rhythm) was already taught in Nights 1-2.
19 min Terminal shield drill -- jab-cross-teep, on shields, 10 pairs
  • Jab-cross into the mitt, no pause
  • Chain the teep immediately after, don't reset in between
  • Control the distance -- the teep should land clean, not crowded or overreached
Setup:
A: Throws a jab-cross combination into the mitt, then immediately chains a lead teep into the kicking shield Partner B presents right after, then recovers to a full, balanced stance.
B: Holds a focus mitt for the jab-cross, with a kicking shield propped ready at their feet, then sets the mitt aside and scoops the shield up with both hands, bracing it, for the teep the instant the cross lands, calling 'ready' before each full sequence.
Reps: 2 rounds of 6 complete sequences per student as the striker (12 sequences total; 12 more as the target holder).
Switch: After Partner A completes 6 full sequences, partners switch roles for B's 6 sequences. Repeat for round 2.
Reset: Both partners reset to a full, balanced stance after each complete sequence before the next.
Equipment: 1 focus mitt and 1 kicking shield per pair.
50% power cap into both targets; target holder must be stable and set before each sequence; no speed escalation beyond clean beginner timing.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Jab-cross-teep volume burst
  • Hard-effort jab-cross-teep volume finisher on the shields already in use tonight.
Setup:
A: Jab-crosses the mitt Partner B holds for 15 seconds, then teeps the kicking shield Partner B braces for the next 15 seconds, for 30 seconds total of maximum controlled volume each round.
B: Holds the mitt for Partner A's first 15 seconds, then sets the mitt down and braces the kicking shield with both hands for the next 15 seconds, then the roles switch.
Reps: 4 rounds x 30s work per role
Switch: Every 30 seconds, immediate switch (7-8 second transition).
Reset: n/a -- continuous work within each round, full stop only at the end of the 5 minutes.
Equipment: 1 focus mitt and 1 kicking shield per pair (the same targets used throughout tonight's Curriculum).
Format: 4 rounds of 75 seconds, exactly 5 minutes. Each round is split into two 15-second phases so the target holder never has to hold the mitt and the kicking shield at once: Partner A jab-crosses the mitt for 15s max controlled volume, Partner B sets the mitt down and braces the shield with both hands, Partner A teeps the shield for 15s max controlled volume, quick 7-second switch, Partner B repeats both phases for 30s, quick 8-second switch/reset, next round begins immediately.
Coach calls: Coach runs a repeating 75-second timer with a mid-round 15-second call for the mitt-to-shield transition, calls 'SWITCH' at each partner interval, counts down the last 10 seconds of the final round.
Scaling: Scale from full-speed combinations to a slower, fully-controlled pace for students whose balance or shoulders fatigue early.
Combinations stay technically controlled even at speed -- no wild, off-balance strikes. Target holder must brace fully every rep, and must have the shield fully braced with both hands before any teep is thrown at it.
