Night 1 — Base Rear Round Kick

Level: Beginner
Objective: Perform a balanced rear round kick with recognizable mechanics.
Equipment checklist: 10 kicking shields, 10 Thai pads
Format: Partner pairs required
Space: Open floor. 4 rows of 5 for Warm Up and the New and Exciting shield-touch relay. 10 partner pairs spread across the floor for the pad terminal drill.
Safety:
  • Round Kick — base mechanics only: This source also teaches a switch-kick extension. STOP after the base rear-kick mechanics for this beginner night — do not teach the switch-stance portion.

Warm Up 8 min

Warm-up menu: run all items or adjust, combine and skip based on the room.

1.0 min Jumping jacks
  • Raise heart rate with a fully generic, universally known movement.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: symmetric, no side distinction
1.0 min High knees in place
  • Drive the knees up at a quick cadence, in place.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: alternating, symmetric
1.0 min Fast feet
  • Quick, light footwork in place.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: symmetric
1.0 min Bodyweight squats
  • Full bodyweight squats at a steady energetic pace.
Setup:
Reps: 12 reps
Sides: symmetric
1.0 min Line touches
  • Jog or shuffle forward to a floor line, touch it, return.
Setup:
Reps: 4 out-and-backs
Sides: symmetric
1.0 min Athletic base + weight shift
  • Find a balanced athletic base and shift weight smoothly, front foot to back foot.
Setup:
Reps: 10 weight shifts, front-to-back
Sides: symmetric weight shift, no stepping side involved
1.0 min Step angle + knee lift + hip rotation flow
  • Lead foot steps out to an angled point, rear-side knee lifts and hip rotates slightly, return to base.
Setup:
Reps: 6 full sequences
Sides: lead foot steps out; rear-side knee lifts and hip rotates (no kick thrown this rung)
1.0 min Single-leg balance check
  • Balance on the rear-side leg for a short count, both legs practiced.
Setup:
Reps: 2 holds each leg, 3 seconds each
Sides: both legs practiced, one at a time

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Partner shield touch-count relay
  • Light, constrained knee-lift-and-touch reps into a partner-held shield, counted for a friendly competitive total.
Setup:
A: Does light, constrained knee-lift-and-touch reps into the shield Partner B holds -- a tap/touch, not a full-power strike -- for a timed 30-second round, counting reps out loud.
B: Holds a kicking shield low at hip/thigh height, braced, calling out the running count with Partner A.
Reps: 3 rounds of 30 seconds per role (touches counted each round)
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch immediately: holder becomes toucher, toucher becomes holder.
Reset: Reset to athletic base between individual touches; full reset to starting position at the top of each new round.
Equipment: kicking shield
Touch/tap contact only -- shield holder must brace before each round begins; no full-power strikes.

Muay Thai Curriculum 35 min

10 min Stance, weight shift and step angle
  • Feet shoulder-width, half-step back on the rear side, knees soft
  • Hands at eyebrow height, elbows in
  • Keep weight shifting front-to-back even when not striking
Setup:
Reps: 3 rounds of (10 weight shifts + 8 lead-foot step-outs)
Sides: lead foot steps out; rear leg stays planted (no kick this rung)
12 min Round Kick — base mechanics only
  • Step to 2 o'clock (not straight out), pull the far arm across as counterbalance
  • Rotate the hip through, strike with the shin — not the foot
  • Reset to stance after every kick
Setup:
Reps: 5 sets of 6 kicks, reset to stance between every kick
Sides: rear leg kicks; lead foot steps to open the angle; whichever stance is the student's natural one (orthodox or southpaw) -- no switching mid-arc
This source also teaches a switch-kick extension. STOP after the base rear-kick mechanics for this beginner night — do not teach the switch-stance portion.
13 min Terminal pad drill — rear kick, 10 pairs
  • Same mechanics as shadow reps, now on pads
  • Reset to stance after every rep
  • 50% power — technical focus, not power focus
Setup:
A: Strikes first. Throws a rear round kick into the pad Partner B holds. Resets to full stance after every single kick before throwing the next.
B: Holds one Thai pad at rib height with both hands, elbows in, weight braced on the back leg. Calls 'ready' before each rep. Resets pad position between reps.
Reps: 2 rounds of 8 rear-leg kicks per student (16 kicks per student total)
Switch: After Partner A completes round 1 (8 kicks), partners switch immediately: B becomes striker, A becomes holder, for B's round 1 (8 kicks). Repeat for round 2.
Reset: Striker resets to full stance after every individual kick; pair resets to neutral facing position before each new round.
Equipment: 1 Thai pad per pair (10 pads total for a class of 20)
50% power cap; pad holder must call 'ready' before each rep so the striker never kicks a target that isn't set.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Squat-jump + mountain-climber intervals
  • Hard-effort interval finisher using two universally known bodyweight movements.
Setup:
Reps: 5 rounds x 40s work (roughly 15-20 reps per work interval at a self-paced effort)
Sides: symmetric, no side distinction
Format: 5 rounds of 40 seconds work / 20 seconds rest, exactly 5 minutes. Rounds 1, 3, 5: squat jumps. Rounds 2, 4: mountain climbers.
Coach calls: Coach runs a visible 40/20 interval timer, calls 'GO' at the start of each work interval and 'REST' at each rest interval, counts down the last 5 seconds of the final round.
Scaling: Squat jumps scale down to bodyweight squats (no jump) for lower-impact students; mountain climbers scale down to slow alternating knee-to-chest taps.
Land softly with soft knees on every jump; self-pace intensity; stop and walk in place if dizzy or short of breath.

Night 2 — Step-In Rear Round Kick

Level: Beginner
Objective: Cover distance with a step-in entry and kick without losing stance.
Equipment checklist: 10 kicking shields, 10 Thai pads, floor lines for 10 individual footwork lanes
Format: Partner pairs required
Space: Open floor. 4 rows of 5 for Warm Up. 10 partner pairs with shields for New and Exciting. 10 individual floor-line lanes for the entry-footwork rung. 10 partner pairs with pads for the compressed review and terminal drill.

Warm Up 8 min

Warm-up menu: run all items or adjust, combine and skip based on the room.

1.0 min Jumping jacks
  • Raise heart rate with a fully generic movement.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: symmetric, no side distinction
1.0 min Lateral shuffles
  • Shuffle side to side, low and athletic.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: symmetric, both directions
1.0 min Reverse lunges
  • Step back into a reverse lunge, alternating legs.
Setup:
Reps: 10 total, alternating legs
Sides: both legs, alternating
1.0 min Fast feet
  • Quick, light footwork in place.
Setup:
Reps: 45 seconds continuous
Sides: symmetric
1.0 min Line touches (forward emphasis)
  • Jog or shuffle forward to a floor line, touch it, return.
Setup:
Reps: 4 out-and-backs
Sides: symmetric
1.0 min Stance + weight shift recap
  • Find the athletic base and shift weight smoothly.
Setup:
Reps: 8 weight shifts, both directions
Sides: symmetric weight shift
1.0 min Forward step + balance hold
  • Lead foot steps forward, land in stance, hold balance briefly, step back.
Setup:
Reps: 6 forward steps with a 2-second balance hold on landing
Sides: lead foot steps forward; rear leg trails and resets (no kick this rung)
1.0 min Knee lift + hip rotate recap
  • Rear-side knee lifts and hip rotates under control.
Setup:
Reps: 8 knee lifts, alternating legs
Sides: both legs practiced, alternating

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Step-to-target reaction drill
  • React to a partner stepping back by closing the new distance and lightly touching the shield.
Setup:
A: Reacts. On the coach's call, steps in with the lead foot to close the new distance and lightly touches the shield with a knee-lift-to-target (light touch, not a full kick).
B: Holds a kicking shield and, on the coach's call, takes one step back to open the distance, then holds still for Partner A's reaction.
Reps: 3 rounds of 8 reactions per role
Switch: After 8 reactions, partners switch roles: holder becomes the reactor, reactor becomes the holder.
Reset: Full reset to starting distance after each reaction before the next call.
Equipment: kicking shield
Touch/tap contact only; shield holder must be set and stable before calling for the next rep.

Muay Thai Curriculum 35 min

6 min Compressed review: base rear round kick (partner pads)
  • Same mechanics as Night 1: step to 2 o'clock, shin strike, reset to stance
Setup:
A: Strikes first. Throws a rear round kick into the pad Partner B holds, resetting to full stance after every kick.
B: Holds one Thai pad at rib height, braced, calling 'ready' before each rep -- the same pad this pair will use for the rest of tonight's Curriculum.
Reps: 4 kicks per student (Partner A kicks 4, switch, Partner B kicks 4).
Switch: After Partner A's 4 kicks, partners switch immediately for Partner B's 4 kicks.
Reset: Striker resets to full stance after every kick.
Equipment: 1 Thai pad per pair (10 pads for a class of 20).
50% power cap; pad holder calls 'ready' before each rep.
12 min Step-in entry mechanics
  • Full step forward, land in stance (not standing tall)
  • Recover to stance after every step
Setup:
Reps: 4 blocks of 8 reps (32 reps total)
Sides: lead foot steps forward; rear leg trails and resets (no kick this rung)
17 min Terminal pad drill — step-in rear round kick, 10 pairs
  • Step forward to launch the kick, don't hop
  • Recover to stance after every rep
Setup:
A: Strikes first. Steps in and throws a rear round kick into the pad Partner B holds. Recovers to full stance after every kick.
B: Holds the same Thai pad from the review rung (already in hand, no equipment change) at rib height, braced. Calls 'ready' before each rep.
Reps: 2 full rounds of 8 rear-leg kicks per student (16 kicks minimum); a 3rd partial round if time allows.
Switch: After Partner A's round of 8, partners switch immediately: B strikes, A holds, for 8 kicks. Repeat for round 2.
Reset: Striker recovers to full stance after every kick; pair resets to starting distance before each new round.
Equipment: Same Thai pad per pair used in the compressed review — already in hand, no equipment change between Curriculum rungs.
60% power cap; pad holder must call 'ready' before each rep.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Shield rear-kick interval sprint
  • Hard-effort rear-kick volume finisher on the shields already in use tonight.
Setup:
A: Kicks the shield Partner B holds for 30 seconds of maximum controlled volume each round.
B: Holds the shield braced for Partner A's 30-second work interval, then the roles switch.
Reps: 4 rounds x 30s work per role (2 minutes of kicking work per student total)
Switch: Every 30 seconds, immediate switch (7-second transition).
Reset: n/a -- continuous work within each round, full stop only at the end of the 5 minutes.
Equipment: 1 kicking shield per pair (the same shields used in tonight's New and Exciting drill).
Format: 4 rounds of 75 seconds, exactly 5 minutes. Each round: Partner A kicks the shield for 30s max controlled volume, quick 7-second switch, Partner B kicks the shield for 30s max controlled volume, quick 7-second switch, next round begins immediately.
Coach calls: Coach runs a repeating 75-second timer, calls 'SWITCH' at each interval, counts down the last 10 seconds of the final round.
Scaling: Scale from full-power round kicks to teep/push-kick volume for students struggling with balance under fatigue.
Kicks stay technically controlled even at speed -- no wild, off-balance strikes. Shield holder must brace fully every rep; coach can call any student to slow down if form degrades.

Night 3 — Check and Return

Level: Beginner
Objective: Check a partner's controlled counter kick and return immediately with a round kick.
Equipment checklist: shin guards for all 20 students, 10 Thai pads, 10 focus mitts (visual cue)
Format: Partner pairs required
Space: Open floor. 4 rows of 5 for Warm Up. 10 partner pairs for New and Exciting and all partner Curriculum rungs. A clear kicking lane per pair for the live check-and-return work.
Safety:
  • Check mechanics (live, portion only): Use ONLY the check-mechanics portion of this source. It also teaches a switch-kick return, which this beginner arc excludes — the return in this arc is always the straight rear kick already taught.
  • Check and return kick: Use ONLY the 'Scale' version of this source's Full-vs-Scale pairing — the 'Full' power version is not appropriate for a beginner class.
  • Final assembled terminal drill: step-in, recover, check, return: Constructed terminal drill — no single source contains this complete sequence; component sources are cited above and only their beginner-appropriate portions are used (FightClub #2921's Level 1 only; its Level 2/3 switch-kick variants are explicitly excluded from this beginner arc).

New and Exciting 12 min

12 min Check-position reaction challenge
  • React fast to a partner's visual raise-cue by rising into the check position, then reset -- counted for a friendly competitive total.
Setup:
A: Reacts. The instant Partner B raises the pad, rises fast into the check position (knee to elbow, shin up), then resets to stance -- counting clean, fast reactions out loud.
B: Holds a focus mitt at their side and raises it quickly at unpredictable moments (not a steady rhythm) as the visual cue -- no kick is thrown, this is a cue only.
Reps: 4 rounds of 30 seconds per role, counting clean reactions
Switch: After each 30-second round, partners switch: cue-giver becomes the reactor.
Reset: Full reset to stance after every reaction before the next cue.
Equipment: focus mitt
No kicks are thrown in this drill — it is cue-and-reaction only, fully non-contact.

Muay Thai Curriculum 35 min

5 min Compressed review: step-in rear round kick
  • Same step-in kick as Night 2, recover to stance every rep
Setup:
Reps: 2 sets of 6 reps
Sides: lead foot steps in; rear leg kicks
None — this is a full re-teach, not a reminder-only review.
7 min Check mechanics (live, portion only)
  • Knee to inside of elbow, shin at 45 degrees, hands stay high
  • Strong base — don't fall over on the check
  • Set the leg back down, no rush
Setup:
A: Checks. Stands in stance, watches the feed, checks the incoming kick with a strong base using the rear-side leg, resets to stance.
B: Feeds. Throws one slow, telegraphed rear round kick at Partner A's lead shin, at 30% power, calling 'kick coming' before each one.
Reps: 10 checks per student (Partner A checks 10 while B feeds, then switch, B checks 10 while A feeds).
Switch: After A completes 10 checks, partners switch immediately for B's 10 checks.
Reset: Both partners reset to full stance after every rep.
Equipment: Shin guards on both partners for the rest of tonight's Curriculum (put on now; stays on through the terminal drill).
Shin guards mandatory before this rung begins; 30% power cap; feeder must call out before every kick.
8 min Check and return kick
  • Check, then return the SAME kick immediately — no pause
  • Scaled power only tonight
Setup:
A: Checks the feed with the rear-side leg, then immediately returns a rear round kick into a pad Partner B raises right after feeding.
B: Feeds one slow kick at 30% power (shin guards), then immediately raises a Thai pad at rib height for Partner A's return kick.
Reps: 8 check-and-return combos per student (A checks+returns 8 times while B feeds+holds, then switch, B does 8).
Switch: After A's 8 combos, partners switch for B's 8 combos.
Reset: Both partners reset to full stance after each combo.
Equipment: Shin guards (already on from the prior rung) plus 1 Thai pad per pair, picked up now and kept through the terminal drill.
Feed stays at 30% power and telegraphed; return kick is pad-only, never partner-to-partner.
15 min Final assembled terminal drill: step-in, recover, check, return
  • A: step-in kick into the pad, recover to stance
  • B: pad down, slow controlled counter kick only
  • A: check it, then return fire into the pad the instant B raises it back up
Setup:
A: Step 1: throws the step-in rear round kick into the pad B is holding, recovers to stance. Step 3: checks B's slow counter kick with the rear-side leg. Step 4: the instant B raises the pad again, returns a rear round kick into it, then recovers to stance.
B: Step 1: holds the Thai pad at rib height for A's step-in kick. Step 2: sets the pad down at their feet in one practiced motion (modeled slowly by the coach first) and throws ONE slow, telegraphed rear kick at A's shin, calling 'kick coming' out loud, 30% power, shin guards on. Step 4: immediately picks the pad back up and holds it for A's return kick.
Reps: 2 rounds of 6 complete 4-step sequences per student as the checker (12 sequences total as checker; 12 more as the counter-thrower/holder).
Switch: After Partner A completes 6 full sequences as the checker, partners switch roles for B's 6 sequences. Repeat for round 2.
Reset: Both partners reset to full stance after each complete 4-step sequence before the next one begins.
Equipment: Shin guards on both partners (already on) plus 1 Thai pad per pair (already in hand from the prior rung) — no new equipment needed for the terminal drill.
Shin guards required for both partners for the entire drill. Counter kick is always slow, telegraphed, and capped at 30% power — this is a timing drill, not sparring. No switch-kick variation. No speed escalation beyond clean beginner timing. Coach paces the room and can hold any pair at a slower step if the choreography isn't clean yet.

Burnout 5 min

5 min Check-hold + return-kick burst (pads only)
  • Hard-effort finisher combining an isometric check hold with an explosive return-kick burst, pad contact only.
Setup:
A: Holds the check position (knee-to-elbow) for 15 seconds, then explodes into 20 seconds of max-effort return kicks into the pad Partner B holds.
B: Holds the pad steady during Partner A's 15-second check hold, then braces for the 20-second kick burst.
Reps: 4 rounds x (15s hold + 20s burst) per role -- roughly 60-90 seconds of kicking work per student total, depending on pace.
Switch: After each 35-second hold+burst sequence, a 5-second switch, then the other partner repeats the sequence.
Reset: Full reset to stance at the top of each new round.
Equipment: 1 Thai pad per pair (already available from tonight's Curriculum).
Format: 4 rounds of 75 seconds, exactly 5 minutes. Each round: Partner A holds the check position for 15s, then explodes into a 20-second max-effort burst of return kicks into the pad Partner B holds, then a 5-second switch; Partner B does the same 15s hold + 20s burst; next round begins immediately.
Coach calls: Coach calls 'HOLD', then 'BURST', then 'SWITCH' every round on a repeating 75-second timer.
Scaling: Shorten the check hold to 10 seconds and/or reduce burst kick power for students whose balance is compromised by fatigue.
Pad-only contact for the entire Burnout — no shin-to-shin contact under fatigue. Pad holder must brace fully before every burst; kicks stay technically controlled even at speed.
