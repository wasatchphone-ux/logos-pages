BL-001 · output waves

Power Wave Bag

“The Tide”

Pilot

Burst-and-recovery structure changes the feel from a nonstop bag sprint.

individuallow impactheavy bagswaves

Five-minute clock

  1. 0:00–1:00 Cycle 1: 15s power, 45s active defense
  2. 1:00–2:00 Cycle 2: 15s power, 45s active defense
  3. 2:00–3:00 Cycle 3: 15s power, 45s active defense
  4. 3:00–4:00 Cycle 4: 15s power, 45s active defense
  5. 4:00–5:00 Cycle 5: 15s power, 45s active defense

Launch in 30 seconds

15-second power combinations alternate with 45 seconds of light movement, guard and defense. Launch words: Power, then move and defend. Stay technical. Five waves.

Students do

Throw a pre-approved two- or three-strike combination hard for 15 seconds, then circle, jab lightly, slip and cover for 45 seconds.

Logistics, scaling and safety

8 / 16 / 24 students

Use 4 pairs, 2 teams of 4, or 8 individual stations as the organization requires.

Use 8 pairs or 4 teams of 4; duplicate stations to prevent queues.

Use 12 pairs or 6 teams of 4; run two parallel clockwise circuits when needed.

Odd number

Create one trio and rotate the third student as holder, counter or active shadow worker; no one sits out.

Equipment shortage

Share stations in teams of three; the off-equipment role performs active technical movement that matches the module.

Beginner / low impact

Easy box-and-move recovery becomes 30 seconds; power is 10 seconds.

Advanced

Power phase becomes 20 seconds while recovery stays technical.

Safety

Stop a rep when stance, target brace, lane spacing or landing control fails. No student-to-student striking and no maximal partner resistance.

Fatigue

upper-body dominant, cardiovascular dominant; Shoulders and calves

Pairs well with

Punch or mixed class

Avoid after

Shoulder-volume or hard bag class

Scoring

Total technically clean power combinations.

Rotation Rules

  1. Never repeat the exact burnout on consecutive classes.
  2. Avoid repeating the same family on consecutive classes.
  3. Avoid the same primary movement pattern more than twice in one week.
  4. Do not use the same exact module within six classes unless requested.
  5. Balance upper-body, lower-body, total-body, partner and team formats.
  6. Use at least one partner or team burnout per week when logistics allow.
  7. Use at least one striking-volume burnout per week.
  8. Use plyometrics selectively rather than every night.
  9. Avoid three consecutive burnouts using the same interval structure.
  10. Track recent use and fatigue overlap before selection.
