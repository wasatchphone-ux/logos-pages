Why these sources were selected

Easton's kickboxing curriculum forms the instructional backbone of the Curriculum block — not because it's data-tier-1, but because it's the only pool in this catalog written as an actual instructor script: slow-motion breakdown, explicit common errors, explicit advice on when a variant is too advanced for the room. Two of its own techniques (Round Kicks, Checking & Returning) bundle a switch-kick extension the source itself treats as a step up in difficulty — this plan uses the base portion only and says so in the notes rather than silently dropping the complexity. FightClub supplies the partner-pad application and the closing constrained drill because that's what its short, camera-ready clips are actually good at, and the agility-ladder drill gives New and Exciting a genuine novelty (new equipment, same kick) instead of a harder technique. The bookends were chosen to prime the exact movement pattern (teep step drill, hip circles) instead of defaulting to generic conditioning.

Rejected candidates

Considered & excludedReason
Teeps + Round Kicks (Easton)wrong difficulty — the source itself says combining a switch kick directly off a teep 'might be a little bit advanced for kickboxing students.'
Wide weight transfer kick (Sylvie #27 Karuhat)redundant — an advanced-distance variation of a stepping kick already covered more simply by 'step back and kick' / 'side step for power kick'; also a time-constraint cut.
Hop Back + 5 Counters Movement Drill (FightClub)insufficient fit — cycles through five different counter tools (punch/teep/kick/lowkick/knee) in one drill, more variety than a single-kick-focused beginner hour has room to support; belongs in the defense/countering class instead.

Why these sources were selected

Tier-1 material anchors the two ends of the Curriculum block that need an explainable, repeatable name — Sylvie's Armadillo Guard opens it and Easton's Redirects closes it, and both come with cues a coach can say out loud without paraphrasing. Everything in between is FightClub, and that's deliberate: it's the only pool in this catalog with clips built explicitly around graduated intensity (Full vs Scale, 3 Levels), which is exactly what 'a scalable base version plus one or more progressions for experienced students' requires — no tier-1 clip in this catalog is structured that way. The warm-up was built to rehearse guard movement and partner reading specifically, so the first 8 minutes already look like a smaller version of the hour to come.

Rejected candidates

Considered & excludedReason
spike guard (Sylvie #58 Jitti Jitti Gym)wrong difficulty and insufficient coaching detail — flagged advanced, no mechanical cues recorded, elite close-range nuance without a teachable entry point.

Why these sources were selected

Tier-1 Sylvie material anchors the two hardest-to-source pieces of this class — genuine posture/frame teaching (clinch frame building + proper clinch posture) and a live-grip entry (arm control clinch entry) — because Easton's kickboxing curriculum has no clinch content at all (it's a striking-only program) and FightClub's clinch clips are almost entirely knee-strike output with no posture-establishment content of their own; this is exactly the case the source-role rule anticipated. FightClub then supplies every strike/turn/sweep/defense step because that's where the catalog's actual clinch action lives — short, clearly partner-drilled segments that slot cleanly into 4-6 minute progression steps. The bookends (sprawl tag, push resistance) were picked for partner-contact carryover into a class that's mostly partner work, rather than generic solo cardio.

Considered & excludedReason
double plum grip (Sylvie #131 Panomtuanlek)wrong difficulty — the source describes it as a lock 'only Panomtuanlek or Dieselnoi ever really hold well,' elite-only with no teachable entry point for an intermediate class.
ultimate arm lock with snake grip (Sylvie #62 Rambaa Somdet)insufficient coaching detail and time constraints — described as an 'advanced arm hook,' no mechanical cues recorded, and too technically dense to introduce on top of a block that already carries six items.
