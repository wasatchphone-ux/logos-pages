01

Executive Summary

What We Are Building

A small internal automated trading operation that runs deterministic intraday strategies on MES (Micro E-mini S&P 500) futures during the U.S. session via Tradovate. The system enforces hard risk limits, logs every decision and trade, evaluates strategies in paper mode, and promotes only proven setups to 1-contract live trading. This is not discretionary. There is no AI during v1. There is no human judgment in the trading loop.

Why MES U.S. Session First

MES is the most liquid, technically clean, well-documented micro futures instrument. The U.S. session (9:30–4:00 PM ET) has the highest volume, tightest spreads, and most reliable price action. Starting here creates a controlled environment where strategy evaluation is not contaminated by session differences, instrument quirks, or overnight gaps. One instrument. One session. One execution environment. Everything gets proven in this lane before anything is added.

Why Scope Is Intentionally Narrow

Multi-market, multi-session, multi-strategy builds fail at the evaluation stage. If three things are changing at once, you cannot determine what is working and what is not. V1 isolates the one variable that matters: whether a deterministic rule-based strategy has real edge on MES during the U.S. session. Every other capability — AI discovery, additional instruments, additional sessions — is blocked until that question is answered by data.

What V1 Success Looks Like

V1 Pass Definition
System runs clean30 paper trading days with no critical execution failures
Risk controls holdDaily loss cap never breached. Kill switch fires correctly in simulation.
At least 1 strategy earns promotionMeets all hard criteria defined in Section 9
Logging worksEvery trade has complete record. No missing fields.
EOD report deliveredTelegram report sent every trading day without manual trigger
Business objective: Generate $100/day net average from automated MES intraday trading. That is approximately $2,000/month. The system gets there by proving edge first, scaling second. No live dollar touches a strategy that has not passed the paper validation gate.

Strategy Specification

Implementation requirement: Every rule below must be implementable as a hard if/else conditional. If a programmer needs to interpret any rule, the rule is incomplete. These are the final rules for the paper evaluation period. They cannot change once paper trading begins.

Strategy 1 — 5-Minute ORB

InstrumentMES continuous contract
Timeframe5-minute bars
Opening range window9:30:00 to 9:35:00 ET. The single candle that opens at 9:30.
OR highHigh of the 9:30 candle (closes at 9:35)
OR lowLow of the 9:30 candle
OR rangeOR high minus OR low in points
Invalid range — skip dayIf OR range < 2.0 points OR OR range > 25.0 points, no trade today for this strategy
Long triggerA candle after 9:35 CLOSES above OR high. Must be a full candle close, not a wick.
Short triggerA candle after 9:35 CLOSES below OR low. Must be a full candle close, not a wick.
Direction filterFor long: price must be above session VWAP at trigger candle close. For short: below VWAP.
EntryMarket order placed at open of next candle after trigger. Not on the trigger candle itself.
Stop — longOR low. Fixed. Does not trail.
Stop — shortOR high. Fixed. Does not trail.
Target — longOR high + OR range. (Entry level + 1× the opening range)
Target — shortOR low – OR range. (Entry level – 1× the opening range)
InvalidationIf price closes back inside the OR after triggering, exit at market immediately. Log as invalidation.
Time-based exitIf trade still open at 11:30 ET, exit at market. No ORB trades entered after 11:00 ET.
Max trades per day1 trade total. First trigger (long or short) wins. No second entry after first trade closes.
No-trade conditionsOR range invalid. No VWAP alignment. Already took trade today. Time past 11:00 ET.

Strategy 2 — VWAP Reclaim / Reject

InstrumentMES continuous contract
Timeframe5-minute bars
VWAP definitionSession-anchored VWAP. Starts at 9:30 ET. Formula: cumulative(((H+L+C)/3) × volume) / cumulative(volume). Resets every session.
Reclaim conditionPrice closed BELOW VWAP for minimum 2 consecutive 5-min candles, then closes ABOVE VWAP
Reject conditionPrice closed ABOVE VWAP for minimum 2 consecutive 5-min candles, then closes BELOW VWAP
Long triggerReclaim condition met. Trigger candle close is the signal.
Short triggerReject condition met. Trigger candle close is the signal.
Minimum cross distanceClose price must be >= 1.0 point above/below VWAP at trigger. If close is within 1.0 point of VWAP, skip — not a clean cross.
EntryMarket order at open of next candle after trigger candle.
Stop — longLow of trigger candle. If distance from entry to stop < 3.0 points, skip trade.
Stop — shortHigh of trigger candle. If distance from entry to stop < 3.0 points, skip trade.
Target1.5 × stop distance from entry. Example: entry 5050, stop 5047 (3 pts), target 5054.5
Time-based exitAny open trade closed at market at 3:45 ET.
No-trade window — openNo trades from 9:30 to 9:45 ET. First 15 minutes excluded.
No-trade window — closeNo new entries after 3:00 ET.
Max trades per day3 total. After 3 trades, strategy is done for the day regardless of outcome.
No re-entryAfter a trade exits, wait for a fresh reclaim or reject condition to form. No chasing.

Strategy 3 — EMA Pullback in Trend

InstrumentMES continuous contract
Timeframe5-minute bars
EMA lengths9-period EMA, 21-period EMA, 50-period EMA. Standard exponential smoothing. Applied to candle close.
Bullish trend condition9 EMA > 21 EMA > 50 EMA on current bar close. AND 9 EMA value is higher than it was 5 bars ago. AND 50 EMA value is higher than it was 5 bars ago.
Bearish trend condition9 EMA < 21 EMA < 50 EMA on current bar close. AND 9 EMA value is lower than it was 5 bars ago. AND 50 EMA value is lower than it was 5 bars ago.
No-trend conditionIf 9 EMA and 21 EMA are within 1.0 point of each other, trend is not clear. No trade.
Valid pullback — longTrend is bullish. Current bar CLOSES at or below the 21 EMA. Current bar CLOSES at or above the 50 EMA. This bar is the pullback bar.
Valid pullback — shortTrend is bearish. Current bar CLOSES at or above the 21 EMA. Current bar CLOSES at or below the 50 EMA. This bar is the pullback bar.
Long triggerThe candle immediately after the pullback bar closes ABOVE the high of the pullback bar.
Short triggerThe candle immediately after the pullback bar closes BELOW the low of the pullback bar.
Trigger windowTrigger must occur on the candle immediately following the pullback bar. If it does not trigger on the next candle, the setup is void. Wait for a new pullback.
EntryMarket order at open of candle following the trigger candle.
Stop — longLow of pullback bar minus 0.5 points.
Stop — shortHigh of pullback bar plus 0.5 points.
Min stop distanceIf distance from entry to stop < 2.0 points, skip trade.
Target2.0 × stop distance from entry. Fixed target. Does not trail.
Time-based exitNo new entries after 2:30 ET. Any open trade closed at 3:45 ET.
Max trades per day2 total per day for this strategy.
No-trade window — openNo entries before 9:50 ET. EMAs need at least 4 candles to initialize.

Risk Engine Specification

MES point value: $5 per index point. Tick = 0.25 points = $1.25. All dollar values below assume 1 MES contract.

Hard Risk Controls

Per-trade max risk$50 max. If stop distance × $5 > $50, reject the signal. Do not reduce position size — reject the trade.
Max open positions1 per strategy at a time. If strategy has open trade, it cannot take new signal until position closes.
Daily loss cap — systemCombined daily P&L across all strategies hits -$100. All 3 strategies halt immediately. No more trades today.
Daily loss cap — strategySingle strategy combined daily loss hits -$60. That strategy halts for remainder of session. Others continue.
Consecutive loss kill3 consecutive losing trades in a session for one strategy. That strategy suspended for rest of day.
Daily profit capOptional. If combined daily P&L hits +$100, system halts. All open positions closed.
Session cutoff3:45 PM ET. All open positions closed at market. No exceptions.
No-entry cutoffEach strategy has its own last-entry time (see Section 4). System enforces these hard.

Execution Safety Rules

Duplicate order preventionBefore placing any order: check open orders for same strategy + same symbol. If found, reject new order and log incident.
Rejected order handlingLog to execution incidents table. Do NOT retry automatically. Wait for next valid signal.
Stale data handlingIf no tick received in 30 seconds during session hours, pause all 3 strategies. Send immediate Telegram alert. Resume only after live tick confirmed.
Bracket failureIf bracket order fails to place stop leg: immediately send stop as separate order. Log as critical incident. Alert Telegram.
Order state desyncOn startup and every 60 seconds: reconcile internal order state against Tradovate open orders. If mismatch, halt strategy and alert.
News windowPreload daily economic calendar. No new entries ±5 minutes around high-impact events (FOMC, CPI, NFP, etc.). Log skipped signals as "news block."
DST handlingAll session times defined in ET. System must convert correctly during DST transitions. Test explicitly for March and November changeover.
Holiday calendarPreload CME holiday calendar. System must not attempt to connect or trade on CME closure days.

Paper Trading Framework

Execution modeTradovate paper account. Real API. Real order routing. Simulated fills. Not a local backtest.
Strategy isolationEach strategy is a separate logical instance. Separate order IDs. Separate P&L tracker. Separate kill switches.
Order conflict preventionAll 3 strategies share one instrument (MES). Total max concurrent open positions: 3 (one per strategy). No strategy sees another's positions.
Fair comparisonAll strategies run same session, same data feed, same clock. No strategy gets preferential treatment.
Rule freezeFrom day 1 of paper period to promotion decision: zero parameter changes to any strategy rule. Violations invalidate the evaluation window.
Evaluation period30 calendar trading days minimum. Excludes CME holidays.
Minimum sample30 completed trades per strategy before promotion decision is valid. If a strategy has fewer than 30 trades at 30 days, extend evaluation until 30 trades reached.
Paper slippage modelTradovate paper fills at market. Add 0.5 points to reported slippage in analytics to model expected live fill degradation.

Validation and Promotion Framework

Rule: No strategy earns promotion by looking good. Every criterion below must be met simultaneously. One failure = no promotion.

PROMOTE — All 6 Must Pass

1. Minimum trade count≥ 30 completed trades during evaluation window
2. Positive expectancyExpectancy > +0.20R. Formula: (win_rate × avg_win_R) + (loss_rate × avg_loss_R) > 0.20
3. Drawdown ceilingTotal paper drawdown for this strategy < $200 over the evaluation period
4. Execution reliabilityFewer than 3 execution incidents attributed to this strategy during evaluation window
5. Rule freezeZero parameter changes made to this strategy during evaluation window. Any change resets the clock.
6. No critical incidentsZero instances of: duplicate fills, runaway position, stuck bracket, state desync causing incorrect position

HOLD — Inconclusive

Trade count < 30Insufficient sample. Extend evaluation. No decision yet.
Expectancy 0.05R to 0.20RMarginal positive. Needs more data. Hold and observe 10 more trades.
1–2 execution incidentsFixable. Address the incident type. Continue evaluation.

DEMOTE — Borderline Failure

Expectancy -0.30R to 0.05R after 30+ tradesWeak or marginally negative. Watch 10 more trades. If still below 0.05R, kill.
Drawdown $150–$200Approaching ceiling. Under observation. No new entries increase unless trending positive.

KILL — Immediate

Expectancy < -0.30R after 30+ tradesKill. Log reason. Do not attempt to fix the strategy mid-evaluation.
Total paper drawdown > $200Kill immediately. Capital preservation.
Execution incidents ≥ 6System is unreliable on this strategy. Kill until root cause resolved.
Any critical execution failureKill immediately. Fix before any re-evaluation.
Any rule change during evaluationEvaluation window is void. Restart paper period from day 1 with frozen rules.
If all 3 strategies are killed: Do not add new strategies. Return to Phase 2. Revise exactly one rule per strategy. Restart paper period. Document what changed and why.

Failure Modes

Failure ModeWhy It MattersHow to DetectHow to Reduce in V1
Bad strategy logic Rules coded incorrectly produce wrong signals Unit test against known historical sessions before paper period Section 10 E08–E10 require signal match on 10 historical sessions
Overfitting Rules tuned to historical data, fail live Live win rate significantly below paper win rate after 30 live trades Rules are fixed before paper period. No tuning during evaluation.
Too-short evaluation window 30 trades is statistically weak. Short-term luck is indistinguishable from edge. High variance in week-over-week expectancy during paper period 30-trade minimum hard floor. Hold decision extends window if sample is small.
Paper / live mismatch Paper fills at ideal prices. Live fills degrade at entry. Live slippage field in trades table. Compare avg live slippage vs paper. Add 0.5-point slippage model to paper analytics. Thin-edge strategies will fail this test.
WebSocket disconnects Stale data leads to bad signals or missed exits Stale data detector: no tick in 30 seconds during session Auto-reconnect logic. Halt trading on stale data. Alert immediately.
Bracket failures Position open with no stop = uncontrolled risk Fill confirmation received but stop leg missing from order state Bracket failure fallback: send stop as separate order. Log critical incident.
Rejected orders Signal fires but no fill. Missed trade or phantom position assumption. Order status check after placement. No fill confirmation = rejected. Log incident. Do not retry. Wait for next signal. Track frequency.
Stale data System acts on old price. Signals based on wrong VWAP or EMA. Tick timestamp delta exceeds 30 seconds Halt all strategies. Alert. Resume only after live tick confirmed.
Strategy overlap conflicts Two strategies simultaneously holding MES in opposite directions. Confusion in P&L tracking. Position tracker: monitor all open positions across strategies Each strategy is independent. System tracks each separately. No cross-strategy netting.
Selection bias Choosing to promote a strategy because it had a good week, not because it met all criteria. N/A — prevented by structure Section 9 promotion criteria are hard and simultaneous. No partial credit.
Overtrading Too many trades per strategy per day. Commissions erode edge. trades_taken column in daily_summary exceeds max_trades per strategy Max trades per day enforced hard in strategy modules. Risk engine blocks excess.
Hidden slippage Slippage consistently 1–2 points beyond expected. Strategy not actually profitable live. slippage_pts field averaged over 30+ trades. If avg > 0.75 points, investigate. Track every fill. Compare expected vs actual. Slippage threshold triggers review.
Rule drift Small "obvious" parameter tweaks during evaluation invalidate the test. SPEC.md version control. Any change = committed change with timestamp. Rule freeze clause in Section 9. Any change resets evaluation clock.
False confidence from short results 5 wins in a row → decision to go live early N/A — prevented by structure 30-trade minimum hard floor. No exceptions. No early promotion.

Hard gate: Nothing below starts until MES U.S. session has completed Phase 9 (20 live days, positive net, stable execution).
Step 1Extend MES U.S. session live to 60 days. Confirm edge is durable across market regimes, not just a hot streak.
Step 2Scale MES to 2 contracts on top-performing strategy. Same rules. Same risk caps per contract.
Step 3Add second strategy to live if it passed promotion and is uncorrelated with live strategy. Run 20-day shadow before going live.
