// Strategy — Read This First

Do not mass-apply. A 2-5% response rate on cold applications means 90 apps gets you maybe 2 callbacks. Pick 5 targets, go deep on each.

Your background in corporate security is a real differentiator here — data centers care intensely about physical access control, badge management, and surveillance. Lead with that. The Allied Universal entry point below is a direct bridge using skills you already have.

Find the recruiter or hiring manager on LinkedIn. Two sentences, direct message. "I'm a corporate security professional transitioning into data center ops — here's why I'm targeting your team specifically." That gets read. Indeed applications mostly don't.

Certs That Move The Needle

Do First
CompTIA A+
Hardware fundamentals. Gets past ATS filters at Oracle and Meta. Required or preferred on most DCT1 postings. Two-part exam, 3–4 months self-study.
Do Second
CompTIA Network+
Networking fundamentals. Opens the systems track inside data centers. Required for most mid-level roles. Pairs well with A+.
Security Track
CompTIA Security+
DoD 8570 compliant — required for most government-adjacent and cleared contractor roles. Direct path to the NSA contractor ecosystem. Leverages existing security knowledge.
After 1 Year In
DCCA / DCIE
Data Center Certified Associate (EPI). Shows specialization. Most employers won't require it for entry, but it's a differentiator for promotion to senior tech or facilities lead.
